About the Crop:

Cotton - Suvin (BT), LRA 5166 is a high-yielding, genetically modified variety known for its pest resistance and superior fiber quality. It thrives in well-drained, loamy to sandy loam soils with a pH of 6.0 to 7.5. The crop requires approximately 800-1,000 mm of water per acre, which can be managed through rainfall or irrigation. Typically, LRA 5166 yields around 1,000-1,500 kg of cotton lint per acre. Profit margins are generally robust, ranging from 20-35%, depending on market prices and input costs.

  • Soil Composition: well drained deep alluvial soils in the to black clayey soils of varying depth in central region and in black and mixed black and red soils in south zone with ph 5.8 to 8.0.
  • Water need per Acre: 2.62 to 3.28 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Soil Degradation, Pest Infestation, Waterlogging
  • Fertilizers: Diammonium Phosphate, Urea, Muriate of Potash , Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Lambda-Cyhalothrin, Acetamiprid, Thiamethoxam.
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹61,800 to ₹1,68,200
